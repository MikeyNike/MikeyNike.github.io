Bill Brown works at the intersection of literary, visual, and material cultures, with an emphasis on "object relations" asking questions of how inanimate objects enable human subjects (individually and collectively) to form and transform themselves and indeed how objects and subjects transform one another. In a short piece called "Thing Theory," he points out how things and thingness might become new objects of critical analysis.

Dislodging the object-thing distinction from fundamental ontology (Heidegger) and from psychoanalysis (Lacan), Brown uses it as a tool for apprehending the unanticipated force of an object, however banal that object may be.
