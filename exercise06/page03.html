When we look across human existence, objects have always been present around us. From the first weapons used for hunting in the pre-settlement times to our modern phones today, these objects have become a huge part of our everyday life and how we navigate the world around us.

However, how many of us truly understand our relationships with these objects or try to be aware of their presence around us?

This level of awareness of how we engage with objects goes beyond viewing objects as purely functional to fulfill a purpose. It goes beyond noticing when they break down or malfunction; when they become “things” and we lose sense of how to interact with them because they disrupt our expectations.
